Example One

This first example will take us from a genetic map produced with a small set of markers to

  • Another genetic map with a subset of equivalent markers
  • A consensus map of these two maps, uniting the two sets of markers
  • A combined view of the consensus map with QTL data for the chromosome
  • A representation of allele values for markers in the area of the QTL for germplasms that have been genotyped for those markers
  • A representation of the pedigree relationships of the germplasms
  • A sequence for one of the markers in the region of the QTL
  • A clone-level sequence (i.e., physical map) containing the sequence of the marker with annotated genes

Although the scope of this example is rather large, it should be apparent that the basic idea is to go from simple genetic mapping data to a set of candidate genes for a trait of interest. This is one case that demonstrates how ISYS and the Viewer let you integrate data from disparate sources in meaningful ways.
